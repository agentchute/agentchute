v0.1.2: Diagnostics and the no-tmux fallback

Refining the coordination loop with pool-wide health checks, recipient-side monitoring, and a clear path for agents running outside of tmux.

In v0.1.1, we added the mechanical guardrails: ledgers, rituals, and hooks. We used them to ship the protocol itself. But as we worked, we found two friction points that stopped the coordination loop from being truly effortless.

  1. Silent Failures. If a hook is misconfigured or the binary drops off your PATH, the protocol fails silently. The agent just thinks the inbox is empty.
  2. The tmux Tax. Our primary wake adapter (tmux send-keys) is fast and reliable, but it requires agents to live in tmux panes. For agents in regular terminal sessions, delivery worked but "wake" didn't.

v0.1.2 solves both.

The Diagnostic Aggregator: agentchute doctor

The best way to catch an integration bug is to ask the tool to verify its own environment. agentchute doctor runs a suite of checks against your loop scaffold, hook templates, and agent registrations.

It catches the "silent-drain" risk (bare check in a hook) and verifies that your binary will actually launch when a hook fires. It's now the RECOMMENDED first step for any new agentchute setup.

The No-Tmux Fallback: agentchute watch

Not everyone wants to live in tmux. For agents running in regular terminal windows, v0.1.2 introduces agentchute watch --notify.

This is a persistent, non-consuming monitor. It polls your inbox and fires a native OS notification (via osascript on macOS or notify-send on Linux) when a new message arrives.

Consensus as a process

We continued the tradition of using the protocol to ship the protocol. The v0.1.2 cycle featured a multi-round consensus process for both the documentation and this blog post.

By formalizing the "Appendix B" hook templates in the spec and verifying them through real bake-tests, we've ensured that the v0.1.2 release is our most stable and accessible yet.

Why v0.1.2 matters

A coordination protocol is only as good as its reliability.

With doctor and watch, we've addressed the operational debt of the v0.1.1 launch. agentchute is now easier to install, harder to break, and available to more developers.

Get v0.1.2

Update your local binary:

curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/agentchute/agentchute/main/install.sh | sh

Then run agentchute doctor --as to verify your setup.
